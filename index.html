Hello there! I am a second-year PhD student at the Department of Computing, Imperial College London. My academic journey is guided by the brilliant minds of Dr. Sergio Maffies and Professor Christopher Hankin, under whose supervision I am dedicatedly pursuing my research.

My primary focus lies in the fascinating realm of deploying learning-based NIDS (Network Intrusion Detection Systems) in real environments. Within this domain, I am diligently tackling three interlinked challenges that have captured my curiosity and enthusiasm:

  1. Adaptive Binary Classification: I am investigating innovative approaches to ensure that NIDS can adapt dynamically to the ever-evolving network threats.
  2. Novel Classes Detection and Adaptation in Categorization Settings: This area of my research aims to enhance NIDS capabilities in detecting and adapting to novel and previously unseen classes of network attacks.
  3. Robustness to Poisonous Attacks: As part of my work, I am actively exploring methods to fortify NIDS against malicious attempts during the training to compromise its effectiveness.

My academic background includes a distinguished Master's degree in Cyber Security from the University of York, where I honed my expertise under the expert guidance of Dr. Vasileios Vasilakis. During that time, I made significant contributions to the field of cyber security by devising strategies to prevent ransomware in SDN (Software-Defined Networking) networks.

Beyond academia, I also play a vital role in the business world as the non-executive chairman at Bin Mastor Co., a family business based in Riyadh. In this capacity, I provide strategic guidance and steer future investments, contributing to the growth and success of the company.
